Payment Request Guide

VR+ Philanthropy Division · Finance managed by VERE Family Office · Last updated 2026

The Process
Five steps, in order. Tap any step to expand. Nothing moves until Step 0 is done.
What this covers
Category A
OpEx
Salaries (non-Deel), software, travel, professional services, office expenses
Category B
Direct Costs
Costs made on behalf of a grantee or sponsored project (not direct transfers)
Category C
Grants
Direct capital transfers to grantee or partner organizations. Always require CEO sign-off.
Out of scope (Phase 1)
Deel Card expenses and personal reimbursements are not covered here. Phase 2 addenda targeting Q2 2026 will address those. If in doubt about the category, ask your Manager before submitting.
0
Before anything else
Approval
Your manager · VR+ CEO for grants and unbudgeted items
▾

You need written approval before assembling any PDF, filling any database row, or sending any email. No exceptions.

Who approves: Authorization levels are defined by dollar amount and role tier in the Authorization Matrix. When in doubt, escalate to the next level.

  • OpEx and Direct Costs: follow the Authorization Matrix thresholds by role
  • Grants: always require written authorization from the VR+ CEO, regardless of amount (interim requirement until the Grants Authorization Policy is finalized)
  • Unbudgeted items or budget variance above 10%: VR+ CEO or Principal sign-off required
Anti-splitting rule
Approval limits apply to the total commitment value. Multiple related purchases or phased contracts for the same engagement count as one transaction. Do not split payments to circumvent approval thresholds.

  • Invoice: from the vendor, confirming service and amount
  • Bank details: beneficiary name, account number or IBAN, bank name. SWIFT/ABA codes are mandatory for all international transfers, regardless of amount
  • Approval email: written confirmation from the authorized approver
Currency note
All payments execute in USD via JP Morgan. If the invoice is in a local currency (COP, BRL, ARS, CRC, etc.), you must document: (a) invoice amount in local currency, (b) FX rate applied, and (c) agreed USD equivalent. JP Morgan receives only the USD amount.

Invoice address: Invoices must be addressed to FVR Ltd. If a vendor cannot invoice to FVR Ltd., you need an exception authorization from the Finance Director before proceeding.

2
Once PDF is assembled
Save to Drive
Upload to your cost center folder · Save the link immediately
▾

Upload the consolidated PDF to your cost center's designated folder in Google Drive.

Save the link immediately after uploading. You'll paste it into the database in Step 3. Google Drive links are based on file ID, not path — moving the file later does not break the link.

Access rules
Each VR+ team member works only within their own cost center folder. Do not upload to another team's folder. The Treasury Analyst may reorganize subfolders freely without invalidating your saved links — do not re-upload a file just because it was moved.
3
After saving to Drive
Fill the Database
All fields required · Correct expense category · USD amounts
▾

Complete the database row for this payment. Every field is required. Incomplete entries may delay payment processing.

  • Select the correct expense category: OpEx, Direct Cost, or Grant. Do not use "Investments" as a category — it is a reporting label only.
  • Enter the USD amount. If original invoice is in local currency, also log the local amount and FX rate in the corresponding fields.
  • Paste the Google Drive link from Step 2 into the document link field.
  • Cost center must match your own — entries for another cost center require explicit authorization.
One payment = one complete row
The database row and the consolidated PDF together form the official record of the payment request. Both must be complete before sending the email.

No informal payment instructions
Verbal instructions, WhatsApp messages, Slack messages, or any informal request for payment execution are not valid and will not be processed. The only valid channel is the written process: database entry plus email to payreq@verefo.com.
After you hit send
You do not need to follow up. VERE reviews on Thursday, payments execute on Friday, and proof of payment is available by Monday noon. If something is wrong with your request, VERE will contact you.

Emergency payments (out-of-cycle)
Emergency approvals do not waive documentation
All supporting documents must be submitted no later than the following Thursday. An emergency payment is subject to the same audit and compliance standards as any standard payment.
1
Written notification (required first) ▾
Notify the next available senior authority in writing (email or messaging app). Verbal notification may precede written confirmation but cannot substitute it. The notification must clearly state: the nature of the urgency, the amount, the vendor, and the cost center.
2
Provisional written approval ▾
The senior authority grants provisional approval in writing (email) to the Finance Director. The provisional approval must include the reason for the exception and explicit authorization for the specific amount.
3
Finance Director assessment ▾
The Finance Director and Treasury Analyst assess operational feasibility. If appropriate, the Finance Director authorizes the out-of-cycle execution. The Finance Director holds final authority over all emergency payment decisions.
4
Formal ratification (within 2 business days) ▾
Formal written ratification must be obtained from the appropriate approving authority within 2 business days of payment execution. The ratification must include the reason for the exception and confirmation that all standard documentation requirements have been or will be satisfied.

The Cadence
OKRs are only as effective as the rhythm built around them. Here's how it works.
Two things keep OKRs alive
Keeping your tasks updated in Profit.co as work happens, and writing a monthly check-in that tells the story behind the numbers. Both are required. Neither replaces the other.

A
Event-driven — every time real progress happens
Update your tasks
You, as work happens. Don't wait for the check-in deadline.
▾

Tasks are the engine of progress in Profit.co. Every time you mark a task In Progress or Completed, the KR progress bar moves automatically.

  • Update task status whenever real progress happens.
  • Attach supporting documentation to the task comment: links to deliverables, reports, or agreements.
  • Keep task comments outcome-focused: what was done, what's next, what's blocked.
  • Store all documents in Google Drive or Notion and link to them from Profit.co. Do not attach files directly.
The subtask trap (most common mistake)
Only parent Tasks move the KR progress bar. Subtasks are for personal organization and don't count toward KR progress. If your work is tracked as a Subtask, it won't appear in KR metrics. Make sure all key deliverables are logged as parent Tasks.
B
Calendar-driven — once a month · Due within 10 days after the end of each month
Write your monthly KR Check-in
Written at the Key Result level in Profit.co, regardless of whether numbers moved. Covers all area tasks and KRs.
▾

The check-in is written at the Key Result (KR) level in Profit.co. Two required elements:

1. Status (traffic light): Not Started / On Track / At Risk / In Trouble / Completed. Pick the one that reflects reality.

2. Narrative comment (mandatory): What advanced since the last check-in, what is delayed or blocked, key risks or dependencies, and a clear rationale for the status you selected.

How to write a strong check-in:

Do this Avoid this
Write the status that reflects reality, not the one you wish were true Selecting On Track when the KR is behind to avoid a difficult conversation
Explain why a number hasn't moved (deprioritized, blocked, or capacity issue) Leaving the check-in blank because nothing changed
Flag blockers clearly so leadership can act on them Writing only "good progress" with no detail or context
Select At Risk early — it's a signal, not a failure Waiting until In Trouble to flag a problem that's been building
Tag relevant stakeholders with @mentions for accountability Leaving ambiguous statements with no clear owner or next step
On the At Risk status
At Risk does not mean failure. It means something needs attention: a blocker, a delay, a flag. Flagging At Risk early is a sign of a healthy, transparent team. Update the status honestly — the context you provide is what enables leadership to help.
Task Comment vs. KR Check-in Comment
Task comment: Point-in-time update — write it when something happens: milestones, blockers, delays, or status changes. Goes on the task itself.
KR check-in comment: Monthly big-picture narrative of overall team progress on the KR. Written in the Check-in tab, not task-by-task.
Bonus tip
Review your tasks in Profit.co before your team meeting. It keeps the session focused and productive instead of using meeting time to catch up on status.

Strategic Review: Objective Check-in in Profit.co
CEO + Leadership team. Bi-monthly session followed by mandatory documentation at the Objective level.
▾
KR Check-in vs. Objective Check-in
The monthly KR Check-in is written at the Key Result level about execution progress (Step B, done by Champions monthly). The Objective Check-in is written at the Objective level about strategic decisions, priority shifts, and course corrections. It is written by Leaders after each bi-monthly Strategic Review session, and it is not automatic: if it's not documented, it did not happen as far as the record is concerned.

Session cycles: Feb-Mar  ·  Apr-May  ·  Jun-Jul  ·  Aug-Sep  ·  Oct-Nov  ·  Dec-Jan

Book the session within the first two weeks of each cycle close. Use the CEO's calendar directly.

Before the session:

  • Read the General Summary Report (sent by the Director of Staff and Operations at least 48h before the session). It covers: overall objective progress, cross-team patterns, strategic risks, and organizational priorities.
  • Review your team's KR data in Profit.co. Know where each KR stands vs. its target.
  • Come prepared to discuss: blockers needing leadership attention, cross-team dependencies, and any priority or resource flags.

During the session:

  • Review KR progress vs. targets across all departments.
  • Surface cross-team risks and coordination needs.
  • Make decisions on priorities and resource shifts for the next cycle.
  • Structural OKR changes (weights, targets, names) are auto-logged in the Activity Log. No separate entry needed for those.

After the session (required, within 48 hours):

  • Open Profit.co and navigate to each Objective reviewed in the session.
  • Write the Objective Check-in on the relevant Objectives (this is at the Objective level, not the KR Check-in tab).
  • Document: decisions made, priority shifts, plan changes, narrative context.
  • Verbal agreements not documented in Profit.co are not part of the official record.
  • Completion window: within 48 hours of the session.
Not documenting = it didn't happen
Undocumented Strategic Review outcomes exist only in memory and create accountability gaps over time. The Activity Log captures structural changes automatically. Everything else (decisions, priorities, course corrections) requires a manual Objective Check-in entry. No exceptions.
Do this Avoid this
Write the Objective Check-in within 48h of the session Relying on the Granola notes or memory as the official record
Document the narrative: what shifted, what was decided, what's next Writing a generic or vague entry that doesn't reflect real decisions
Enter it at the Objective level in Profit.co Confusing this with the monthly KR Check-in (those are at the KR level)
Treat the 48h window as a hard deadline Leaving it for later and losing the context of what was decided
Configuration Reference
D
When creating or editing a KR
Set the correct KR date range
KRs must reflect the actual work period, not the system default quarter.
▾

Profit.co defaults to the system quarter, which does not always match when the work actually happens. Set the date range manually for any KR that does not perfectly align with a standard quarter.

When to configure a custom date range:

  • The KR spans more than one quarter (multi-period).
  • The KR starts after the beginning of the quarter.
  • The KR closes before the quarter ends.
  • The KR covers the full annual period.

How to configure it in Profit.co:

  • Create or open the KR for editing.
  • Expand Ownership and Progress Tracking.
  • Locate KR Date Range.
  • Select the actual start and end date.
  • Save with Update.
Why it matters
A KR with the wrong date range will show as overdue or inactive before or after its real period. Accurate dates keep progress calculations and review windows correct.

Pitfalls & Status Guide
The four most common mistakes — and how to avoid them. Plus the full status reference.
Common pitfalls
⚠️ The subtask trap ▾
Work tracked as Subtasks does not move the KR bar — it becomes invisible progress. Your work exists, but the metrics don't see it.

Fix: Log all key deliverables as parent Tasks. Subtasks are for personal workflow only.
⚠️ Missing or late check-ins ▾
A KR with no check-in gets flagged as Ghost Risk — it looks At Risk even if execution is going perfectly well.

Fix: Treat the check-in as a calendar event. Write it monthly regardless of whether numbers moved.
⚠️ Progress without evidence ▾
Updating a task without documentation means progress is unverifiable and institutional memory is lost. What is not evidenced cannot be verified.

Fix: Always link to evidence (Drive or Notion) in the task comment.
⚠️ Holding On Track too long ▾
When problems are flagged too late, leadership makes decisions on optimistic data. By the time In Trouble is selected, there's little runway left to act.

Fix: Select At Risk early. It is a system signal, not a failure.
⚠️ Parent KR not configured as Progress Tracked (Percent) ▾
When a KR has Sub-KRs, it must be set to Progress Tracked (Percent). Without this, Sub-KR progress does not roll up into the Parent KR automatically and the metric will be inaccurate.

Tasks placed directly inside a Parent KR are for internal organization only — they do not affect KR progress. If tasks need to drive a KR's numbers forward, create a separate Task-Tracked KR and add it as one of the Parent's Sub-KRs.

Fix: Open the KR, go to Edit, expand Ownership and Progress Tracking, and set the tracking method to Progress (Percent).

Keep in mind
Parent Tasks move the KR bar — Subtasks don't.  ·  Check-in is a calendar obligation: write it even if nothing changed.  ·  At Risk early = healthy team. At Risk late = problem.  ·  Evidence lives in Drive/Notion — link it, never attach to Profit.co.

How does my Performance Evaluation work?

Every quarter. What + How. Honest feedback in both directions. · Last updated April 2026

The Cycle
Why it matters, what it covers, and when meetings happen.
The "What + How" Framework
The What
OKR Achievement

Did you deliver on your objectives?

Measured through Profit.co.

The How
Behavioral & Cultural Alignment

Did you show up in a way that reflects our values?

Measured through the six Culture Deck behaviors.

Both pillars matter equally
Delivering results while undermining the team's culture is not strong performance at ver+. Equally, embodying our values while consistently missing objectives means you need support to grow. The conversation is qualitative: no scores, no ratings, just specific examples and honest reflection.
The six behaviors from our Culture Deck

Take Full Responsibility · Roll Up Our Sleeves · Build with Rigor · Speak Up · Do the Right Thing · Stay Focused.

These are the operating system of how we work, and they will be a live part of every evaluation conversation.

Your 5 Steps
Tap any step to expand. Steps cover both the Team Member and the Manager.
1
First thing, before anything else
Look Back
Both: Team Member and Manager
▾

Team Member: Look at the development areas you committed to. Ask yourself whether you executed on the priorities you agreed. Reflect honestly on where you have improved and where you still need to grow.

Manager: Read the team member's previous evaluation in full. Assess whether the agreements and development commitments were followed through, whether the priorities were executed, and where genuine improvement has happened versus where gaps remain. Come with specific examples.

Why this step matters
The evaluation meeting is not where your manager sees your work for the first time. The previous quarter's commitments are the reference point for this quarter's conversation.
2
Before the meeting
Fill Your Templates
Team Member: 3A + 3C · Manager: 3B-T1 or 3B-T2
▾

If you are the Team Member, complete:

Template 3A (Self-Evaluation): quarter context, 3 key accomplishments, 3 areas for improvement, 3 priorities for next quarter, and the support you need from your manager.

Template 3C (Upward Feedback): honest feedback on your manager's leadership, communication, and support.

If you are the Manager, complete:

Template 3B-T1 (for Tier 1: CEO, VP, Directors) or 3B-T2 (for Tier 2: Managers, Coordinators, Associates). Cover OKR summary, behavioral alignment, the tier-specific dimensions, strengths, and development areas. Always grounded in concrete examples.

Upward feedback is attributed
Your name is connected to your feedback. This is a direct expression of our Speak Up and No Masks values. Managers are expected to receive feedback with openness and professionalism.
3
3 business days before the meeting
Submit & Schedule
Team Member submits 3A + 3C · Manager schedules within 15 days of Q end
▾

Team Member: Send your completed 3A and 3C to your manager at least 3 business days before the meeting. Late submission undermines the conversation.

Manager: Schedule the meeting so it lands within 15 calendar days after the end of the quarter. Send the calendar invite with the Google Meet link. Review the team member's 3A when it arrives, note where you agree, where your view differs, and which points you want to explore.

4
The meeting (~80 minutes)
The Meeting
Manager leads · Both attend prepared · Recorded via Google Meet + Granola
▾

Moment 1 (15 min): OKR Review in Profit.co. Walk through the quarter's OKR data together. Manager documents decisions and context in the Notes tab.

Moment 2 (~65 min): Performance Review 1:1. Self-evaluation, manager feedback, upward feedback, next-quarter alignment. All in the same meeting.

See tab ③ The 80-min Meeting for the full segment-by-segment agenda.

Recording protocol
The manager starts the Google Meet recording and Granola at the very beginning. Both parties verbally acknowledge the recording. The recording runs for the full meeting; do not pause or stop mid-session. This is a standard requirement at ver+ to ensure accuracy and consistency.
5
Within 2 business days after the meeting
Document & Close
Manager uploads everything · Both own the next-quarter priorities
▾

Manager: Upload all documentation to the team member's evaluation folder in the shared drive (path: /Evaluations/[Team Member Name]/[Quarter]/):

Completed Template 3A (Self-Evaluation) · Completed Template 3B-T1 or 3B-T2 (Manager Feedback) · Completed Template 3C (Upward Feedback) · Google Meet recording link · Granola notes and transcript.

Then notify the Director of Staff and Operations that the cycle is complete.

Both: The 3 priorities you agreed for next quarter are now the reference point for the next cycle. They are the first thing you both look at three months from now.

If you disagree with the documented outcome
Use the Team Member Response section in Template 3B to share your perspective. If you feel that the documentation does not accurately reflect what was discussed, raise it directly with the Director of Staff and Operations.
The 80-min Meeting
Times below are a guide, not a rigid schedule. The goal is for everyone to come prepared and make the most of the time together.
5 min
🎙️ Opening + Recording start
Manager leads
Manager starts Google Meet recording and Granola. Both parties verbally acknowledge the recording. Manager sets a constructive, open tone.
15 min
📊 OKR Review (Moment 1)
Manager (data-led)
Open Profit.co together. Walk through each objective: progress, blockers, impact. Manager documents decisions in the Notes tab (not Check-in).
15 min
📝 Self-Evaluation
Team Member presents
Team member walks through 3 accomplishments, 3 development areas, and 3 next-quarter priorities. Shares support needed from the manager. Manager listens and asks clarifying questions only.
15 min
💬 Manager Feedback
Manager presents
Manager shares their qualitative assessment: OKR summary, behavioral observations, development areas, and strengths. Grounded in concrete examples from the quarter.
15 min
🔄 Upward Feedback
Team Member presents
Team member shares feedback on the manager's leadership, communication, and support, directly and constructively. Manager receives with openness, does not defend or minimize in the moment.
10 min
🎯 Next Quarter Alignment
Both
Together agree on the team member's 3 priorities for the next quarter. Written down during the meeting (not after). They become the reference point for the next cycle.
5 min
✅ Wrap-up + Recording stop
Manager leads
Manager summarizes key takeaways, confirms that templates will be uploaded within 2 business days, and stops the recording.
Key principles for the meeting
Both parties come prepared. Recording starts before any substantive conversation. The self-evaluation segment is led by the team member; the manager listens and asks questions, not challenge or correct. Upward feedback is received with the same openness expected when giving feedback. Agreed next-quarter priorities are written down during the meeting, not after.

FAQ & Help
The most common questions, plus where to go when something goes wrong. Tap any item to expand.
Frequently asked questions
When does my first evaluation cycle start? ▾
The first cycle is Q2 2026 (April to June 2026). Evaluation meetings must take place between July 1 and 15, 2026.
What if I joined ver+ recently? ▾
If you have been with the team for less than 4 weeks at the end of the quarter, you are exempt from that cycle and will participate fully starting next quarter. If you have been here for 4 weeks or more, you will have a prorated evaluation focused on behaviors and early contributions.
Is upward feedback really shared with my manager's name? ▾
Yes. Upward feedback at ver+ is attributed, meaning your name is connected to your feedback. This reflects our Speak Up and No Masks values. Managers are expected to receive feedback with openness and professionalism. Any retaliation for upward feedback is a serious policy violation.
What if I disagree with the feedback I received? ▾
You have a dedicated space in the Manager Feedback Template (3B), Team Member Response section, to share your perspective. If you feel the documented outcome does not accurately reflect the conversation, raise it directly with the Director of Staff and Operations.
Does this evaluation affect my salary? ▾
The evaluation dimensions connect to the Variable Compensation framework currently under development. Until that policy is finalized, no compensation changes will be made solely based on evaluation outcomes without a separate communication from leadership.
What if my manager changes during the quarter? ▾
The new manager leads the evaluation, with input from the previous manager where relevant. The Director of Staff and Operations will coordinate transitions to ensure no one falls through the cracks.
How should I prepare for my evaluation meeting? ▾
Come with concrete examples of your work, key accomplishments, challenges, and honest reflections on both strengths and development areas. Review your goals and the previous quarter's evaluation beforehand. Your preparation directly determines the quality of the conversation.
What if my workload or priorities changed significantly during the quarter? ▾
Evaluations should take context into account. If priorities shifted during the quarter, the conversation should reflect how you adapted, communicated, and contributed under those circumstances, rather than measuring strictly against the original plan.
How is "Behavioral Alignment" assessed in practice? ▾
Behavioral alignment is evaluated based on observable actions and consistent patterns aligned with the six behaviors in the Culture Deck. Feedback must always be grounded in concrete examples. Vague impressions are not sufficient (this includes both positive and critical feedback).
What if I feel my manager doesn't have enough visibility into my work? ▾
Proactively share context, results, and challenges throughout the quarter. The evaluation meeting should not be the first time your work is fully visible to your manager. If you feel this is a recurring issue, raise it directly during the upward feedback section.
What happens to my evaluation records? ▾
Records are stored confidentially in the shared drive under your name. Only you, your manager, and the Director of Staff and Operations have access. Records are retained for at least two years, including after a departure.
What if I don't want my evaluation meeting to be recorded? ▾
Recording evaluation meetings is a standard requirement at ver+. It ensures accuracy, consistency, and proper documentation. Participation in recorded evaluation conversations is part of being a member of the organization.
If something goes wrong
You feel feedback was disrespectful or unprofessional ▾
All feedback is expected to be delivered with professionalism and respect, in line with ver+ values. If you experience behavior that feels disrespectful, personal, or inappropriate during an evaluation, raise the concern with the Director of Staff and Operations. These matters are taken seriously and will be addressed accordingly.
Concern about retaliation for upward feedback ▾
Contact the Director of Staff and Operations immediately. Any retaliation for upward feedback is treated as a serious conduct issue and is escalated directly to the CEO. It is documented formally.
You missed the self-evaluation deadline ▾
Reach out to your manager as soon as possible. The meeting may be rescheduled or held without the self-evaluation. Repeated failure to submit on time is noted in your evaluation record.
The meeting wasn't scheduled by day 8 of the cycle window ▾
The Director of Staff and Operations will follow up directly with the manager. If no meeting is confirmed by day 12, the issue is escalated to the manager's direct supervisor or the CEO.
You disagree with the documented evaluation record ▾
Contact the Director of Staff and Operations with a written description of the discrepancy. They review the recording and Granola notes and facilitate a resolution conversation between the manager and the team member.

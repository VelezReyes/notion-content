Purpose & Scope

This SOP provides step-by-step operational guidance for running the VélezReyes+ quarterly performance evaluation cycle. It is the practical companion to the Performance Evaluation Policy — use the policy for "why," and this document for "how."

This SOP applies to all full-time employees of ver+ and the vere team, across both role tiers:

Tier 1 — Leaders: CEO, VP, Directors Tier 2 — Contributors: Managers, Coordinators, Associates

The process is organized into five phases. Each phase has clear owners, steps, and timing. Everyone involved should read the phases relevant to their role before the cycle begins.

Phases — Step by Step
Five sequential phases. Steps are checkable — mark them as you execute.
Phase 1
Cycle Launch
Who: Director of Staff and Operations · When: First week of the evaluation window (within 5 days of quarter end)

The Director of Staff and Operations is responsible for opening each quarterly evaluation cycle. Their role is to ensure that every team member and manager knows the cycle has started, has access to all the materials they need, and understands exactly what they must do and by when.

Shared Drive Folder Structure
Each team member has a dedicated folder: /Evaluations/[Team Member Name]/
Inside: subfolders by quarter — e.g., Q2 2026/
Each quarterly subfolder must contain: Self-Evaluation (3A) · Manager Feedback (3B) · Upward Feedback (3C) · Meeting Recording · Granola Notes
Steps — click to mark complete
  • 1

    Confirm evaluation window dates

    Confirm the evaluation window dates for the quarter (e.g., Q2 2026: July 1–15).

  • 2

    Share all five materials with the full team

    Distribute via shared drive or email: Template 3A (Self-Evaluation), 3B-T1 (Manager Feedback — Tier 1), 3B-T2 (Manager Feedback — Tier 2), 3C (Upward Feedback), and Guide 3D (OKR Review Reference). Include a brief written explanation for each: what it is, who completes it, and when it is due.

  • 3

    Send the cycle-launch message to all team members and managers

    Message must include: evaluation window dates · self-evaluation submission deadline (3 business days before each meeting) · reminder that all meetings must be completed within 15 calendar days of quarter end · links to all templates · Director of Staff and Operations contact for questions.

  • 4

    Verify shared drive folders are organized and ready

    Confirm that all team member folders are in place and ready to receive completed documentation — one subfolder per team member.

  • 5

    Mid-cycle follow-up with managers (around day 8)

    Follow up with managers to confirm that evaluation meetings are scheduled before the deadline.

Phase 2
Template Completion
Who: Team Member + Manager · When: Before the evaluation meeting — self-eval due 3 business days prior

Once the cycle is launched, each person completes the templates relevant to their role. All templates must be completed before the evaluation meeting — the quality of the conversation depends on the quality of the preparation.

⚠️ Preparation is not optional
Arriving at the evaluation meeting without completed templates undermines the entire process. The team member's self-evaluation should reach the manager at least 3 business days in advance. The manager's feedback must be drafted before the meeting — not improvised during it. Both parties should have reviewed the previous quarter's evaluation before sitting down.
Team Member — What You Complete
  • 1

    Review your previous quarter's evaluation first

    Before writing anything, look at the development areas you committed to, whether your agreed priorities were executed, and where you have genuinely improved.

  • 2

    Complete Template 3A — Self-Evaluation

    Fill in all sections: quarter context, 3 key accomplishments, 3 areas for improvement or development, 3 priorities for next quarter, and support needed from your manager.

  • 3

    Complete Template 3C — Upward Feedback

    Complete all sections honestly. This feedback is attributed and will be shared directly with your manager during the meeting.

  • 4

    Submit both templates to your manager

    Submit completed Templates 3A and 3C to your manager at least 3 business days before the scheduled meeting.

Manager — What You Complete
  • 1

    Review the team member's previous quarter evaluation in full

    Assess: Were commitments and development agreements followed through? Were agreed priorities executed? Where has there been real improvement — and where do gaps remain? Come to the meeting with specific examples.

  • 2

    Review the team member's completed Template 3A when it arrives

    Note where you agree, where your view differs, and any points you want to explore in the conversation.

  • 3

    Complete the correct Manager Feedback template

    Use Template 3B-T1 for Tier 1 (CEO, VP, Directors) or 3B-T2 for Tier 2 (Managers, Coordinators, Associates). Complete all sections: OKR summary, behavioral alignment, tier-specific dimensions, strengths, and development areas.

  • 4

    Keep Guide 3D accessible during the meeting

    Template 3D (OKR Review Guide) is a reference for documenting in Profit.co Notes tab — keep it open throughout the session.

  • 5

    Schedule the evaluation meeting

    The meeting must take place within 15 calendar days of the end of the quarter. Send a calendar invite with the meeting link.

Phase 3
The Evaluation Meeting
Who: Manager (leads) + Team Member · When: Within 15 calendar days of quarter end · Duration: ~80 minutes

This is a single ~80-minute meeting that combines the OKR Review and the Performance Review. The times below are a guide — not a rigid schedule. The goal is for everyone to come prepared and make the most of the time together.

The manager leads the meeting and is responsible for starting the recording, following the agenda, and maintaining a constructive and safe environment for honest conversation.

Meeting Agenda
TimeSegmentWho LeadsWhat Happens
5 min 🎙️ Opening + Recording Manager Start Google Meet recording and Granola. Both verbally acknowledge the recording. Manager sets a constructive, open tone.
15 min 📊 OKR Review Manager (data-led) Open Profit.co together. Walk through each objective — progress, blockers, impact. Manager documents decisions and context in the Notes tab (not Check-in).
15 min 📝 Self-Evaluation Team Member presents Team member walks through their 3 accomplishments, 3 development areas, and 3 next-quarter priorities. Shares support needed from manager. Manager listens and asks clarifying questions.
15 min 💬 Manager Feedback Manager presents Manager shares qualitative assessment: OKR summary, behavioral observations, development areas, and strengths. Must have reviewed previous evaluation beforehand.
15 min 🔄 Upward Feedback Team Member presents Team member shares feedback on their manager directly and constructively. Manager receives with openness. This is a core part of the ver+ Speak Up culture.
10 min 🎯 Next Quarter Alignment Both Together agree on the team member's 3 priorities for the next quarter. These are documented in the Manager Feedback Template during the meeting — not after.
5 min 🏁 Closing Manager Summarize key takeaways. Confirm that all templates will be uploaded by the manager within 2 business days. Stop recording.
Recording Protocol — click to mark complete
  • 1

    Start Google Meet recording at the very beginning

    Manager starts the recording before any substantive conversation begins.

  • 2

    Activate Granola to capture notes and transcript

    Manager activates Granola before the session begins.

  • 3

    Both parties verbally acknowledge the recording

    "I confirm that this meeting is being recorded." Both participants state this aloud.

  • 4

    Keep recording running for the full meeting

    Do not pause or stop the recording mid-session.

  • 5

    Stop recording at the end and confirm the file is saved

    Manager stops and verifies the file before closing the meeting.

Key Principles for the Meeting

✓ Expected behavior

Both parties come prepared — templates completed, previous evaluation reviewed, Profit.co data checked
Self-evaluation segment led by the team member — manager's role is to listen and ask clarifying questions
Upward feedback received with the same openness expected when giving feedback
Agreed next-quarter priorities written down during the meeting — not after

✗ Avoid this

Manager challenging or correcting during the self-evaluation segment
Defending or minimizing upward feedback in the moment
Improvising manager feedback — it must be drafted before the meeting
Leaving priority alignment for after the meeting — agree and document it during
Phase 4
Post-Meeting Documentation
Who: Manager + Director of Staff and Operations · When: Within 2 business days of the meeting

Once the meeting is complete, the manager is responsible for uploading all materials to the shared drive and notifying the Director of Staff and Operations. This step creates the official record of the evaluation.

File path
All completed materials go to: /Evaluations/[Team Member Name]/[Quarter]/
Do not download recordings locally — share the link directly from the source so the record stays accessible and linked.
Manager — Steps
  • 1

    Share the Google Meet recording and Granola notes to the shared drive

    Share the recording link and Granola notes/transcript to the team member's evaluation folder. Share directly from the source — do not download locally.

  • 2

    Finalize the Manager Feedback template

    Complete any open sections — specifically "Agreed Next Quarter Priorities," which is filled in during the meeting. Use 3B-T1 for Tier 1 and 3B-T2 for Tier 2.

  • 3

    Upload all completed materials to the team member's folder

    Upload to /Evaluations/[Team Member Name]/[Quarter]/: Completed Template 3A · Completed Template 3B-T1 or 3B-T2 · Completed Template 3C · Google Meet recording link · Granola notes and transcript.

  • 4

    Notify the Director of Staff and Operations of completion

    A short message confirming that documentation for this team member's cycle is complete is sufficient.

  • 5

    Repeat for each direct report

    Complete the upload and notification process for every team member who had an evaluation this cycle.

Director of Staff and Operations — Steps
  • 1

    Confirm receipt of all documentation for each team member

    Verify that each manager has submitted the full documentation package for their team members.

  • 2

    Verify that the folder structure is complete and correctly organized

    Check that each team member's folder contains all required documents in the correct subfolder.

  • 3

    Track overall cycle completion — follow up by day 15

    Follow up with any manager who has not confirmed completion by day 15.

  • 4

    Flag any missing or incomplete documentation and escalate if needed

    Refer to the Escalation Path section for the correct handling steps.

Phase 5
HR Review & Follow-Up
Who: Director of Staff and Operations · When: Within 5 business days of cycle close (all documentation received)

Once all documentation is received and the cycle is closed, the Director of Staff and Operations conducts a full review of the evaluation records across the team. This is not an administrative check — it is a substantive review of the quality, consistency, and content of the feedback given and received.

The purpose of this review is to identify whether any situation requires a follow-up conversation — with a manager, a team member, or leadership — before the next quarter begins.

What HR visibility means at ver+
The Director of Staff and Operations does not evaluate team members — that is the manager's role. Their job in this phase is to be the guardian of the process: ensuring feedback is fair, honest, specific, and followed through. If something doesn't look right, they have both the responsibility and the authority to ask questions and follow up.
Step 1 — Read Every Evaluation

Review the full set of documentation for each team member: self-evaluation, manager feedback, upward feedback, and Granola notes. For each person, check:

  • A

    Significant gap between self-evaluation (3A) and manager assessment (3B)?

    Does the gap need to be discussed with the manager to ensure the team member received a fair explanation?

  • B

    Upward feedback revealing leadership concerns?

    Look for patterns of poor communication, lack of support, or behaviors inconsistent with our culture.

  • C

    Recurring development areas with no improvement cycle after cycle?

    Is the manager providing the right support, or is the plan not working?

  • D

    Did the team member follow through on previous cycle commitments?

    If not, was that addressed in the evaluation by the manager?

  • E

    Is the manager's feedback specific, constructive, and honest?

    Or is it vague and disconnected from reality? Are there any red flags — team members at serious risk, or dynamics that could escalate?

Step 2 — Decide Whether a Conversation Is Needed

Based on the review, determine whether a follow-up conversation is needed and who it should involve:

SituationWho to Talk ToPurpose
Large gap between self-evaluation and manager feedback Manager Understand the manager's reasoning. Ensure the team member received a fair and clear explanation during the meeting.
Upward feedback reveals a leadership concern Manager Private conversation to share the pattern observed. Not accusatory — focused on development and awareness.
Recurring development areas with no progress Manager + Team Member Explore whether the improvement plan is realistic and whether the right support is in place. May lead to a more structured development plan.
Team member seems disengaged or at risk Manager first, then Team Member if needed Understand root causes. Determine if the issue requires a retention conversation, additional support, or escalation.
Manager feedback is vague, generic, or appears dishonest Manager Coach the manager on feedback quality. In serious cases, ask them to revise the documentation.
Systemic pattern across a team (e.g., everyone scores similarly low on a behavior) Manager + Leadership Flag as a team-level issue that may require a broader intervention or calibration conversation.
Step 3 — Have the Conversation

If a follow-up conversation is needed, the Director of Staff and Operations initiates it directly. The conversation should:

  • 1

    Frame it as a follow-up to the evaluation cycle — not a disciplinary action

    Set the right tone from the start. This is a development and quality-assurance conversation.

  • 2

    Ground it in specific observations from the documentation

    Never vague or based on rumor. Reference the specific records you reviewed.

  • 3

    Have a clear purpose: clarify, coach, support, or escalate

    Know the intended outcome before going in.

  • 4

    Document with a brief note saved to the relevant folder

    Record a brief note in the shared drive folder for the relevant team member.

Step 4 — Close the Cycle
  • 1

    Confirm all documentation is complete and correctly stored

    Verify the shared drive is fully organized across all team members for this quarter.

  • 2

    Note any open action items to track into the next quarter

    Capture items that need to be visible at the start of the next cycle.

  • 3

    Communicate systemic patterns or team-level concerns to leadership

    If the review surfaced recurring patterns or team-level issues, bring these to leadership before the next cycle begins.

Escalation Path
Most issues in the evaluation cycle can be resolved by the Director of Staff and Operations with a timely follow-up. Here's how to handle the most common situations.
01
Team member misses self-evaluation deadline

First action: Director of Staff & Ops notifies manager. Manager reaches out to reschedule or proceed without it.

→ If unresolved: Noted in the evaluation record. Repeated failure addressed in the following cycle's review.
02
Manager has not scheduled the meeting by day 8

First action: Director of Staff & Ops follows up directly with the manager.

→ If unresolved: Escalated to the manager's direct supervisor or CEO if no meeting is confirmed by day 12.
03
Documentation not uploaded within 2 business days

First action: Director of Staff & Ops sends a reminder to the manager.

→ If unresolved: Flagged in the shared drive as incomplete. Escalated if outstanding after day 17.
04
Team member disputes the evaluation record

First action: Team member contacts Director of Staff & Ops with a written description of the discrepancy.

→ If unresolved: Director of Staff & Ops reviews the recording and Granola notes. Facilitates a resolution conversation between manager and team member.
05
Retaliation concern related to upward feedback

First action: Team member contacts Director of Staff & Ops immediately.

→ If unresolved: Treated as a serious conduct issue. Escalated directly to CEO. Documented formally.

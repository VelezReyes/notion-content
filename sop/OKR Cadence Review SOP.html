Standard Operating Procedure

Company Level
Monthly Message — All Team
Who: Director of Staff and Operations (prepares) · All team members (receive) · When: First week of each month

The monthly company message is a curated, asynchronous update sent to all team members via Slack. Not a meeting. A summary that ensures the entire organization has the same strategic picture at the same time.

Key reminder
This message replaces the All Hands meeting for monthly OKR updates. Content should be digestible in under 3 minutes. Prioritize what changed, what is at risk, and what is next. Send window: first week of the month.
Steps — click to mark complete
  • 1

    Pull OKR data from Profit.co

    Gather the previous month's data: overall progress per team, KR highlights, risks flagged, and main blockers reported in check-ins.

  • 2

    Compile the monthly message

    Content must include: overall OKR progress across teams, how each team contributed to institutional objectives, main risks or blockers, and priorities for the coming period.

  • 3

    Format as visual infographic or concise report

    A clear, readable snapshot — not a data dump. The goal is one single picture everyone can understand in under 3 minutes.

  • 4

    Distribute via Slack

    Send in the first week of the month. Tag the appropriate channels to ensure all team members receive it.

  • 5

    File a copy in the shared drive

    Archive under the corresponding month folder to maintain a historical record of company-level OKR communications.

Documentation rule
Outcomes — decisions, priority shifts, plan changes — must be documented in the Objective Check-in within 48 hours. Verbal agreements not documented in Profit.co are not part of the official record.
Before the Session
  • 1

    Prepare the General Summary Report

    Director of Staff and Operations pulls data from Profit.co: overall objective progress, cross-team patterns, strategic risks, and organizational priorities.

  • 2

    Distribute report to leadership

    Deliver to CEO and leadership team at least 48 hours before the session.

  • 3

    Each leader reviews their team KR data

    Come prepared to discuss blockers, open decisions, and priority flags. No surprises in the session.

During the Session

The session covers the following in order:

#Review item

3Risks or blockers needing leadership attention

  • 2

    Complete documentation within 48 hours

Team Level
KR Monthly Check-in — Champions & Contributors
Who: Champions (VPs/Leaders) — required · Contributors — when applicable · When: Monthly, per cadence calendar, regardless of task movement

The monthly KR Check-in is the team-level ritual that keeps Key Result health visible to the organization. While Profit.co tracks task completion automatically, the check-in adds the human layer: status, narrative, and context no algorithm can provide.

Why this is mandatory
A percentage score tells us how much work was done. The check-in tells us the health of that work. Without it, leadership is navigating with half the information. This is a calendar obligation — write it monthly regardless of whether numbers moved.

StepWhat HappensWhoWhenWhere

Step A — Managing Tasks (The Data Input)

Tasks are the engine of OKR progress. Every parent Task marked In Progress or Completed moves the KR progress bar. Task hygiene is non-negotiable.

⚠️ Critical: The Subtask Trap
Subtasks do not move the KR progress bar. They are strictly for personal workflow organization. All key deliverables must be logged as parent Tasks. If your work is tracked as a Subtask, it is invisible to the KR progress calculation.
  • 1

    Update task status when real progress happens

    Do not wait for the check-in cycle. Update as you work — when a task moves to In Progress or is Completed.

  • 2

    Attach supporting documentation

    Link to the completed deliverable in Google Drive or Notion. Reference it clearly in the task comment: "See attached dashboard — April results."

  • 3

    Verify your work is logged as a parent Task, not a Subtask

    If it's a Subtask, it does not count toward KR progress. Convert key deliverables to parent Tasks.

  • 4

    Keep task comments outcome-focused

    What was done, what is next, what is blocked. Not an activity log — a progress narrative.

Step B — Performing the Monthly Check-in

Written at the Key Result level in Profit.co. Two required elements:

ElementWhat to Include
1. Status (Traffic Light) Not Started   On Track   At Risk   In Trouble   Completed
2. Narrative Comment (mandatory) What advanced since the last check-in · What is delayed or blocked · Key risks or dependencies · Clear rationale for the selected status

✓ Do this

Write the status that reflects reality, not the one you wish were true
Explain why a number has not moved — deprioritized, blocked, or capacity issue
Select At Risk early — it is a signal, not a failure
Tag stakeholders with @mentions for accountability

✗ Avoid this

Selecting On Track when the KR is behind to avoid a difficult conversation
Leaving the check-in empty because nothing changed
Writing only "good progress" with no context or detail
Waiting until In Trouble to flag a problem that has been building
On the At Risk status
At Risk does not mean execution failure. It means a check-in is pending or a flag needs attention. Early At Risk status is a signal of a healthy, transparent team — not a problem team.
  • 1

    Open the KR Check-in tab in Profit.co

    Navigate to the relevant Key Result and open the Check-in feature.

  • 2

    Select the traffic light status

    Choose the status that reflects the real health of the KR — not the one that looks best on a dashboard.

  • 3

    Write the narrative comment

    Cover: what advanced, what is delayed, key risks or dependencies, and clear rationale for the selected status. This is the human insight no algorithm provides.

  • 4

    Save and confirm the check-in is recorded

    Verify that the Check-in History reflects the new entry with the correct date and status.

OKR Review Segment — How to Run It
Notes tab — core rule
Notes go only on KRs where something changed or a decision was made. If a KR is on track with no changes — leave it without a note. Notes exist to create a record of what happened, not to summarize what did not.
  • 1

    Open Profit.co before the meeting

    Have the team member's KR dashboard visible. Also keep Guide 3D (OKR Review Guide) open as a reference throughout the session.

  • 2

    Review each active KR together

    Progress vs. target, check-in history, tasks completed since last review, and open blockers.

  • 3

    Identify KRs where a note is needed

    Note only KRs where something changed, a decision was made, or a plan was modified. On-track KRs with no changes require no note.

  • 4

    Write the Notes tab entry in Profit.co

    Use the format from Guide 3D: Date & Review Period · Progress Summary · Key Achievements · Blockers · Decision or Change · Direction for Next Quarter.

  • 5

    Record the full meeting via Google Meet

    Granola generates meeting notes and transcript automatically. Ensure the recording is active before the session begins.

  • 6

    Upload completed templates to the shared drive

    Templates 3A, 3B, and 3C — plus the meeting recording — go into the team member's folder: /Evaluations/[Name]/[Quarter]

Common Pitfalls
The six most frequent mistakes in the OKR cadence — and how to avoid them.
01
The Subtask Trap

Work tracked as Subtasks does not move the KR progress bar — creating invisible progress that makes healthy execution look flat on the dashboard.

→ Log all key deliverables as parent Tasks. Subtasks are for personal organization only.
02
Missing or Late Check-ins (Ghost Risk)

A KR without a recent check-in appears At Risk in Profit.co — not because execution is failing, but because the system has no narrative update. This misleads leadership about actual health.

→ Treat the check-in as a monthly calendar event. Write it regardless of whether numbers moved.
03
Progress Without Evidence

Task updates without attached documentation are unverifiable. Institutional memory is lost and the record becomes unreliable for performance conversations.

→ Attach documentation to every task update. Link to Drive or Notion — never attach directly to Profit.co.
04
Undocumented Strategic Review Outcomes

Decisions made in the Strategic Review that are not documented in Profit.co exist only in memory. Over time this creates accountability gaps and conflicting recollections.

→ Document all outcomes in the Objective Check-in within 48 hours. If it's not in Profit.co, it did not happen.
05
On Track Status Held Too Long

Maintaining On Track when a KR is behind allows leadership to make decisions on optimistic data. Problems surface too late to address effectively.

→ Flag At Risk early. It is a system signal — not a judgment call or a failure. Early flags enable early support.
06
Blank Notes After Operational Review

Individual OKR decisions made in the 1:1 that are not logged in Profit.co make the evaluation record incomplete — creating gaps that surface at the next cycle.

→ Write Notes entries during or immediately after the meeting using Guide 3D as reference.
